Stage I · Chromatin Clearing

Pioneer Engagement

Pioneer factors utilize non-specific electrostatic interactions with the phosphodiester backbone to rapidly scan condensed chromatin in three dimensions.

Upon encountering their target sequence, their DNA-binding domain inserts an alpha-helix into the nucleosome surface to securely anchor the protein, mechanically distorting the DNA duplex to force approximately 25 base pairs to unwrap from the histone octamer.

Stage II · Transcription

RNA Polymerase II Assembly

The massive TFIID complex undergoes a large-scale structural rearrangement, swinging its mobile lobe A over the promoter DNA to physically deposit the TATA-binding protein (TBP).

With DNA melted by the TFIIH translocase, RNA Polymerase II locks onto the template strand, initiating processive elongation and transcribing genetic code into a nascent, single-stranded pre-mRNA transcript.

RNA POLYMERASE II TRANSCRIPTION BUBBLE PRE-mRNA SYNTHESIS
Stage III · Ribosome Assembly

Cytoplasmic Entry & Initiation

Mature, spliced mRNPs undergo translocation through selective nuclear pores, driven by Dbp5 cytoplasmic stripping.

In the cytoplasm, the 40S small ribosomal subunit clamps onto the mRNA track, scanning for the AUG start codon, before recruiting the massive 60S large subunit to assemble the fully active 80S translation factory.

NUCLEAR EXPORT RIBOSOME ASSEMBLY START CODON POSITIONING
Stage IV · Polypeptide Formation

Elongation & Folding

Elongation factor G (EF-G) drives the ratchet-like translocation of tRNA molecules from the A-site to the P-site.

As the raw polypeptide chain extrudes from the exit port, the Trigger Factor (TF) chaperone immediately engulfs it in a hydrophobic cradle, coordinating with downstream DnaJ/DnaK to fold the protein into its final 3D functional structure.
